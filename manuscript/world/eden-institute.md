# Eden Institute (Memory Tech)

## Purpose
The corporation that invented memory technology approximately 100 years ago. Originally a place of scientific innovation that changed the world. Now the dominant force in memory technology — society depends on them.

## Power and Reach
- Invented the technology the entire justice system relies on
- Government eventually took control of the technology (nationalised? — [TBD])
- But the Institute retained significant influence, resources, and scientific expertise
- Can offer things the government can't: money, cutting-edge medical care, research facilities
- Have agents/operatives who approach people, offer money, gather intelligence
- Pursue memory transfer technology secretly — their ultimate goal

## Key Figures
- [EXECUTIVE NAME] — Head of the Institute. Silver-tongued, predatory, performs civilisation. A snake in a suit.
- The agent with the scar on his hand — approaches Alina, offers money for information
- Evie's father — recruited by the Institute as a scientist to continue transfer research

## Public Perception vs. Reality
- Public: benevolent scientific institution, beacon of progress, school field trip destination
- Children are in awe of it. Alina remembers field trips there.
- But the shine has faded. Scandals: a scientist who was wiped, people at trial who disappeared.
- The copper on the building is tarnishing — literal and metaphorical decay
- Reality: pursuing memory transfer at any cost. Covering up failures. Manipulating the justice system.
- "Progress doesn't wait" is their philosophy — even when it should.

## Role in the Mystery
- Faked Parker's death at the wiped facility to take custody of her as a test subject
- Recruited Evie's father after Maya brought them Steers' research
- Funded the memory transfer experiments
- Want Parker back to study her — the only successful transfer subject
- Will try to extract the transfer knowledge from Alina by force in the final act

## Physical Spaces
- HQ: Glass and steel tower. Once-shiny copper now fading (Statue of Liberty parallel — death of values). Lobby with secretary. Executive's office high up. Basement: labs, medical rooms, holding cells. More guards than scientists below ground.
- Evie's father's lab: small, hidden, tucked away. Eventually burned down (by the father, faking his death).
