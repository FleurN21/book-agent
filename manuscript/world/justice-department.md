# Justice Department

## Purpose
Government body that administers the memory-based justice system. Oversees watchers, analysts, inspections, and the wiping process. Supposed to provide checks and balances on the Eden Institute.

## Power and Reach
- Legal authority over criminal proceedings involving memory technology
- Jurisdiction over criminals awaiting justice (including Parker)
- Can compel handover of prisoners from the Institute
- But losing ground — the Institute has more resources, more money, more scientists
- Steers has been the main force holding them accountable, but she's only one person

## Key Figures
- Amanda Steers — Head of Justice Department. Alina's hero. James's boss/mentor.
- James [LAST NAME] — Rising star watcher, Steers' protege
- Other analysts and watchers (not yet named)
- Staff loyalty is compromised — some take money from the Institute, believing they serve the greater good

## Public Perception vs. Reality
- Public: the institution of justice. Steers is a known public figure — gives speeches, appears in court hearings.
- Steers is seen as a crusader against Institute overreach.
- Reality: Steers has her own dark secret (Project Maya). The department is understaffed and outmatched. Loyalty is fractured.

## Role in the Mystery
- Steers takes custody of Parker from the Institute
- Provides Alina access to the investigation (recordings, interrogation room, analyst equipment)
- The investigation into Parker is run from here
- Steers' arrest creates a power vacuum that the Institute exploits

- Steers' office — where strategic conversations happen
- Analyst hall — investigative machines, headsets. Here the work is investigative, not just confirmatory.
- Holding cells — where Parker is kept
