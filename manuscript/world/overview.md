# World Overview

## Setting
A near-future world where memory technology has fundamentally transformed society, particularly the justice system. The exact time period is not specified but it is at least 100 years after the invention of memory technology.

## The Old World vs. The New
- Detectives were replaced by watchers and catchers
- Courtrooms were replaced by inspection chambers
- Evidence-based trials were replaced by direct memory examination
- The old system was flawed: guilty could go free, innocent could be convicted
- Memory tech was supposed to be perfect: the criminal's own memory as proof
- Alina reads old detective stories — romanticises the old way. Heroes that no longer exist.

## The Eden Initiative
- Philosophy: violence is a product of nurture and difficult circumstances
- Wiping gives criminals a second chance — start from scratch, rebuild
- Society largely accepts this, though some protest (think it's too lenient)
- The initiative assumes people can be reformed if given a blank slate
- Parker challenges this assumption — wiped and still killed again (or did she?)

## Social Fabric
- The wiped are a visible part of society — recognisable by fading tattoos
- They fill service roles. Are known for being docile, gullible.
- Public opinion is mixed: some see them as redeemed, others as getting off easy
- Memory tech has made life easier and better for most people — hard to question something you depend on
- "You can accept anything when you need it to survive" — mirrors our relationship with technology

## Decay and Tarnish
- Things were shinier once. The copper is fading.
- The Institute was once a beacon of progress. Now it's something else.
- Government took control of the tech but lost leverage over time.
- Scandals have eroded trust, but dependency prevents real accountability.
- The world is not dystopian in the traditional sense — it's functional, comfortable, and slowly rotting from within.

## Season and Atmosphere
- The wiped facility area: frozen roads, ice, a river. Cold, isolated.
- The city: institutional, clinical, functional. Not beautiful but operational.
- The tone is institutional — offices, labs, control rooms, holding cells. Not open spaces.
