# Memory Technology — Canonical Rules

## Watching (Memory Inspection)
- Watchers enter a criminal's mind through paired pods to find confessional memories
- Mind-to-mind connection; watcher guides the subject to the relevant memory
- Watcher provides prompts (e.g., "Show me the memory of you killing [name]")
- Subject's emotional/obsessive memories tend to surface naturally
- Subjects can "flicker" between memories if stressed, making the watcher's job harder
- Sessions are recorded for analyst review
- Grounding details (specific questions about the event) help stabilise the connection
- Deep memories are dangerous — too many connections, can cause overload
- "Why" questions trigger deep memories and are against protocol — very dangerous
- Overload = too many memory projections in the queue at once = madness (what happened to James)
- The process feels anti-climactic from the observation room — you can't see what's happening, just monitors and metrics

## Wiping (Memory Erasure)
- Injection of a silvery liquid (like moonlight, shifting light) — the wipe vial
- Attacks the memory centres of the brain [specific mechanism TBD]
- Result: complete erasure of all memories. Subject can still walk, talk, basic functions remain
- Freshly wiped: docile, blank-faced, drooling, flat eyes, can't speak. Like adult children.
- Over time through reintegration therapy: regain speech, basic reasoning, ability to work
- But higher reasoning, complex personality = gone. Known for being gullible.
- Often fill service roles (servers etc.) after reintegration
- Tattoo system: W stamp on the cheek with a specific hue. Designed to fade over time until invisible = "redeemed"
- Fresh tattoos: bright red. 3 years old: still visible. Years later: nearly invisible.
- Grade system: Grade D = community service. Grade A = wiping (violent crimes: murder, etc.)
- Minimum age for wiping: 16
- Three-month rule: inspection must happen within 3 months of arrest, or the option for a memory watch expires. Can still be punished on existing evidence, but won't be wiped without absolute certainty from watching.

## Memory Transfer (The Secret Technology — NOT public knowledge)
- Transfers one person's complete memories into a wiped person's empty mind
- Requires a wiped mind (no existing memory infrastructure to conflict with)
- Must be done fresh — not too far along in reintegration, or the new memories won't integrate properly
- If done on someone who already has memories: overload, madness (like what happened to James, and what Steers did to her test subject)
- The recipient initially believes they ARE the original person
- Over time, especially if kept awake and aware, the recipient may realise they are a copy
- This technology is the central secret of the novel — no one can be allowed to have it
- Implications: the wealthy/powerful could achieve immortality by transferring into wiped bodies. The wiped become a resource to be harvested.

## The Machines
- Pods: enclosed chambers where watcher and subject are placed. Cocoon-like feel from inside.
- Emergency exit button: small button near the feet, not well-known. Parker used it to exit her pod.
- Headsets: used by analysts at their desks to review recorded sessions
- Monitors: heart rate, brain function, connection stability. Visible in the control room.
- Control room: behind a two-way mirror. Operators monitor the session. Recording equipment.
- Analyst floor: sea of headsets in deathly quiet. Clock on the wall. Stamp and paper system for verdicts.
- Wipe vial: silvery liquid, shifting light like moonlight. Injected via needle.

## Limitations and Rules
- Three-month rule (see Wiping section)
- 24-hour minimum between sessions to avoid permanent damage to the subject
- Deep memories are dangerous — more connections = higher overload risk
- "Why" questions are forbidden during inspection
- Session recordings are assigned randomly to analysts for review
- Analysts stamp verdicts: "guilty" + grade (D through A)
- Recordings can be difficult if the subject is flickering between memories
