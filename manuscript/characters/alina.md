# Alina [LAST NAME]

## Role
Protagonist / narrator (first-person)

## Basics
- **Age:** Early-to-mid 20s
- **Appearance:** Not extensively described in outline. Details TBD.
- **First impression:** Sharp eyes, tense posture, someone holding themselves together by force of will

## Personality
- **Core trait:** Fierce loyalty — will do anything for the people she loves
- **Flaw:** Jealousy and self-isolation. Pushes people away, then resents them for the distance.
- **Fear:** Losing the people she loves (has already happened twice — Evie, then James)
- **Want:** Justice for Evie. To see the killer punished.
- **Need:** To let go. To stop defining herself by loss and guilt.

## Voice
- **Speech patterns:** Blunt and direct when strong. Stutters and trails off when vulnerable. Rare dry humour that doesn't quite land.
- **Sample line:** "She was my whole world."
- **What she won't say:** "I'm fine" and mean it. Can't lie to people who know her well.

## Relationship to Memory Technology
- **Role in the system:** Analyst (Petty Crimes). Former Violent Crimes analyst. Failed watcher candidate.
- **Institutional allegiance:** Justice Department (by employment). Independent by nature.
- **Wipe status:** Never wiped. Wipes herself at the end with the vial.
- **What she knows about memory transfer:** Discovers it through the investigation. By the end, she understands how it works — and this knowledge is what she must destroy.

## Mystery Role
- **Connection to the central mystery:** Evie was her best friend. She was present when Parker attacked James. She is the only civilian who can connect the tea shop memory to Evie.
- **What she's hiding:** Nothing from the reader (she's the POV character). From other characters: her growing doubts about Steers, her conversations with Parker.
- **Key scenes:** Every chapter. The inspection (Ch 1), seeing James (Ch 4), tea shop revelation (Ch 13), Parker's room scenes, Steers' confession, the final wipe.
- **What Alina believes vs. what's true:** Believes Steers is a perfect hero (learns she's a murderer). Believes Parker is a monster (learns she's a victim). Believes Evie told her everything (learns Evie's father was involved in the conspiracy).

## Relationships
- **Evie:** Her whole world. Found family. Alina was Evie's caretaker as she got sicker. The grief is raw and all-consuming.
- **James:** Former academy rival, best friend, possible love interest. She's jealous of his success but loves him deeply. His madness is her fault (she asked him to be the watcher). Romantic tension that was never realised.
- **Steers:** Hero worship → working partnership → trust → betrayal → complicated understanding. Steers is the mentor she always wanted. Learning Steers is a murderer devastates her.
- **Parker:** Hatred → curiosity → horror → empathy → something like recognition. By the end, Alina sees Evie in Parker and chooses not to wipe her.
- **The Executive:** Pure antagonism. He represents everything wrong with unchecked power.

## Arc
- **Start:** Grieving, isolated, working a job she hates, defined by loss. Wants revenge.
- **Middle:** Activated by the investigation. Finds purpose, develops relationships (Steers), confronts her jealousy and guilt.
- **End:** Chooses to sacrifice herself rather than let the technology exist. Moves from revenge to justice to selflessness. The final act is choosing that no one — including herself — should have this power.
