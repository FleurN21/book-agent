# Amanda Steers

## Role
Mentor / authority figure / fallen hero

## Basics
- **Age:** Older than Alina (40s-50s? — [TBD])
- **Appearance:** Smaller in person than on TV. A touch of femininity — pink lipstick, or a dress with flowers, or a shirt that contrasts with her authority. Presents the same level of power whether in a suit or a dress. Walks with an assurance Alina can only envy.
- **First impression:** Mythic. Then smaller. Then human.

## Personality
- **Core trait:** Conviction. She does what she believes is right, even when it's hard.
- **Flaw:** Hubris. She started the memory transfer research. She believes her judgment is sufficient to make impossible choices.
- **Fear:** Her past catching up with her (it does).
- **Want:** To contain the transfer technology. To protect the world from what she helped create.
- **Need:** To face what she did — and accept the consequences.

## Voice
- **Speech patterns:** Clipped, authoritative, speaks in absolutes. Doesn't waste words. Rare warmth.
- **Sample lines:** "I was wrong." / "We've seen enough." / "Not over the phone."
- **What she won't say:** The full truth, until forced. She protects by withholding.

## Relationship to Memory Technology
- **Role:** Head of the Justice Department. Oversees the memory-based justice system.
- **Institutional allegiance:** Justice Department. Adversary of the Eden Institute.
- **Wipe status:** N/A
- **What she knows:** Everything. She started Project Maya — the memory transfer research. She knows what Parker is. She knows the Institute wants the technology.

## Mystery Role
- **Connection:** She started it all. Project Maya was her attempt to save her dying friend Maya by transferring Maya's memories into a wiped person. The experiment failed (broke the subject, like James). She gave up. Maya didn't — took the research to the Institute.
- **What she's hiding:** Project Maya. Her past experiment. The murder she committed (the test subject she broke). Her knowledge of what Parker really is.
- **Key scenes:** Ch 3 (rescues Alina from the Executive), Ch 4 (takes Alina to see James), Ch 5 (conversation outside), Ch 7 (interrogates Alina), Ch 8 (shows Alina the memory), Ch 12 (the bluff), Ch 14 (car ride), Ch 20 (tries to look in Parker's head), arrest scene, prison confession.
- **What Alina believes vs. true:** Alina worships her. Steers is also a murderer. She is both the hero and part of the problem.

## Relationships
- **Alina:** Sees potential in her (James told her about Alina). Develops genuine care. Tries to protect her. Ultimately trusts her with the truth.
- **Maya:** Best friend. Died of cancer 7 years ago. Matching necklaces (A and M). The grief that drove Steers to create the transfer technology.
- **James:** Her protege. His destruction hits her hard.
- **The Executive:** Institutional nemesis. He's silver-tongued; she's blunt. Mutual contempt.
- **Parker:** A complicated responsibility. Steers made the technology possible. Parker is the result.

## Arc
- Introduced as an untouchable hero. Gradually revealed as deeply human and deeply flawed. Arrested for murder. In prison, she confesses everything. Her arc is the story's thematic spine: even the best people will sacrifice others to save the ones they love.
