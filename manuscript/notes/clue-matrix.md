# Clue Matrix

## The Solution

Parker has all of Evie's memories transferred into her wiped mind. She was never truly wiped — the Eden Institute faked her death at the wiped facility and gave her to Evie's father as a test subject for memory transfer. Evie's father was recruited by the Institute after Maya (Steers' dying friend) brought them Steers' abandoned research. Parker initially believed she was Evie, then realised she was a copy, and killed the real Evie believing she could replace her. Steers started the original research (Project Maya), experimented on a wiped person (broke them like James), and gave up — but Maya betrayed her promise and took the research to the Institute. The technology allows copying a person's complete memories into a wiped mind. This must never become known or accessible.

## The Reveal Chain

The order in which Alina (and the reader) assembles the truth:

1. **Ch 8:** Parker has a memory involving Alina at the tea shop — why?
2. **Ch 13:** The tea shop in the memory hasn't been renovated yet → memory is 3+ years old
3. **Ch 13:** Parker was wiped 3 years ago → she shouldn't have pre-wipe memories → she was never truly wiped
4. **Ch 17:** Parker was reported dead in a road accident → the wiped facility covered up her survival
5. **Ch 19:** The fake graduation record + the scar → the Eden Institute orchestrated the cover-up
6. **Ch 20:** "Project Maya" → something Steers is hiding, something that frightens her
7. **Ch 24:** Alina rewatches the memory, notices she's smiling AT Parker → recognition → it's Evie's memory
8. **Ch 24:** If Parker has Evie's memories, someone transferred them → memory transfer technology exists
9. **Steers' confession:** She started the research to save Maya, experimented and failed, gave up — but Maya took it to the Institute
10. **Parker's revelation:** She was given all of Evie's memories, believed she was Evie, realised she wasn't, killed the real Evie thinking she could take her place
