# Thematic Threads

Track how themes are developed across the novel. Each theme should be present in at least 3 acts.

## Ethics of Progress
- **The question:** When does technological advancement become moral catastrophe?
- **Act I:** The memory system works. It's better than the old way. Alina accepts it.
- **Act II:** Cracks show. The Institute covers up failures. Progress has a price.
- **Act III:** The Executive's pitch — immortality for the wealthy, the wiped as a harvested resource. "Progress doesn't wait."
- **Resolution:** Alina destroys the knowledge. Progress must wait.
- **Key line:** "Progress doesn't wait." — said approvingly by the Executive, understood as horror by Alina.

## Grief and Letting Go
- **The question:** How do you live after losing the person who was your whole world?
- **Act I:** Alina is frozen. Picture frames turned down. Can't function. Wants revenge as a substitute for processing.
- **Act II:** Slowly activates through investigation. Finds purpose. But grief is always there — the dam metaphor.
- **Act III:** Discovers Evie wasn't perfect. Her father was involved. Evie kept secrets. This doesn't diminish love — it humanises it.
- **Resolution:** Alina lets go of everything, including herself. The wipe is the ultimate act of letting go.

## Identity
- **The question:** Who are you without your memories? Is a copy of a person that person?
- **Parker's arc:** She was given Evie's memories. Believed she was Evie. Realised she wasn't. Became something else — neither Parker nor Evie.
- **The wiped:** Blank slates. Adult children. Can they truly start over? Are they the same person?
- **Alina's choice:** She wipes herself. She ceases to exist. Is the body that remains still Alina?

## The Price of Love
- **The question:** What would you sacrifice to bring someone back? And who would you sacrifice?
- **Steers:** Sacrificed a wiped person's sanity trying to save Maya.
- **Evie's father:** Sacrificed Parker's identity trying to save Evie.
- **The Executive:** Would sacrifice the wiped as a class for the powerful.
- **Alina:** "I would do anything." But when the time comes, she sacrifices herself rather than someone else.
- **The nuclear analogy:** You'd press the button to level a city. But could you kill one person with a knife while looking them in the eye?

## Hero Worship and Disillusionment
- **Alina's poster of Steers** as a teen → meeting the real woman → discovering the truth
- **Steers is smaller in person** — literally and metaphorically
- **The disillusionment is not a betrayal.** Steers is still brave, still principled, still fighting. She's also a murderer. Both are true.

## Justice vs. Revenge
- **Start:** Alina wants Parker to suffer. Wants to see her go down.
- **Middle:** Shifts from wanting pain to wanting answers. Why?
- **End:** Refuses to wipe Parker. Helps her escape. Chooses justice for everyone — even the person who killed Evie.

## The Value of Every Human Life
- **The wiped:** Criminals, yes. But they're still people. Are they?
- **Parker:** A copy. An experiment. A murderer. Still a person.
- **The Executive's logic:** The wiped are already gone. They don't deserve to start over when "better" people could use those bodies.
- **Alina's answer:** "No one should get to decide that."
