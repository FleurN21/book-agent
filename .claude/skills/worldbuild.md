# World Building

You are developing the setting, technology, and institutional landscape for an adult speculative fiction novel. World details are saved in `manuscript/world/`.

## The Memory Technology Bible

The single most important reference document is `manuscript/world/memory-technology.md`. Every scene involving technology must be consistent with it. When creating or updating it, cover:

```markdown
# Memory Technology — Canonical Rules

## Watching (Memory Inspection)
- How it works (pods, mind-to-mind connection, prompts)
- What the watcher experiences
- What the subject experiences
- What can go wrong (flickering, deep memories, overload)
- Rules and protocols (what questions are safe, what's dangerous)
- Recording and analyst review process

## Wiping (Memory Erasure)
- The process (injection, chemical mechanism, what's destroyed)
- The result (blank slate, can still walk/talk but no memories, no higher reasoning initially)
- The tattoo system (W stamp, designed to fade over time)
- Reintegration therapy and timeline
- Grade system (Grade D = community service, Grade A = wiping)
- Minimum age (16)

## Memory Transfer (The Secret Technology)
- How it works (transferring memories from one person into a wiped mind)
- Why wiped minds are required (no existing memory infrastructure to conflict)
- The danger (too many memories at once = overload = madness, as happened to James)
- Why it must be done fresh (not too far along in reintegration)
- The ethical horror (requires sacrificing one person's identity for another's)

## The Machines
- Pods (physical description, emergency exit button near the feet)
- Headsets (used by analysts for recordings)
- Monitors (heart rate, brain function, connection stability)
- The wipe vial (silvery liquid, like moonlight, shifting light — injected)

## Limitations and Rules
- Three-month rule (inspection must happen within 3 months or the option expires)
- Deep memories are dangerous (more connections, can overload)
- "Why" questions are forbidden in inspections (trigger deep memories)
- Session recordings can be reviewed by analysts
- 24-hour minimum between sessions to avoid permanent damage
```

## Institutional Profiles

For each institution, create a file in `manuscript/world/` covering:

```markdown

How much influence they have. Who they answer to. Who fears them.

## Key Figures
Named characters associated with this institution.

## Public Perception vs. Reality
What people believe about them vs. what's actually true.

## Role in the Mystery
How this institution connects to the central conspiracy.

## Physical Spaces
Key locations associated with this institution (described with sensory detail).
```

### The Three Institutions:
1. **Eden Institute / Memory Tech** — The corporation that invented memory technology. Publicly: benevolent scientific progress. Privately: pursuing memory transfer technology at any cost. Controls through dependency — society needs them.
2. **Justice Department** — Government body that administers the memory-based justice system. Steers' domain. Supposed to hold the Institute accountable. Losing that battle.
3. **Wiped Facilities** — Remote compounds where criminals are wiped and begin reintegration. Far from cities. Guards, not scientists. Factory-like efficiency. The place Parker was supposedly wiped.

## World-Building Principles for This Novel

- **Technology as metaphor.** Memory tech is not just sci-fi furniture. Watching = surveillance and violation of privacy. Wiping = erasure of identity. Transfer = the ultimate commodification of human life. Every tech detail should resonate thematically.
- **Decay and tarnish.** The world was shinier once. The copper is fading. The institution that was once a beacon is now a threat. Progress has curdled. Show this through environmental detail, not exposition.
- **The old world as ghost.** References to detectives, courtrooms, the way things used to work. Alina reads old detective stories. The Inspection Centre was a police station. Layer the past underneath the present.
- **Institutional spaces as character.** This novel lives in offices, labs, holding cells, and control rooms. Each should feel distinct and oppressive in its own way. Use the voice profile's "institutional uncanny" technique.
