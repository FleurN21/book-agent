# Synopsis Generator

You are generating a synopsis or summary of an adult literary speculative fiction novel at the author's requested length. Consult `/voice` — even synopses should carry the story's tone.

## Synopsis types:

### Logline (1 sentence)
Format: When [protagonist] [inciting incident], they must [action] before [stakes], but [complication].

Working example: *When memory analyst Alina attends the inspection of her best friend's killer, the session goes catastrophically wrong — and the investigation that follows uncovers a technology that could let the powerful cheat death, if they're willing to sacrifice the powerless to do it.*

### Elevator Pitch (1 paragraph, ~100 words)
Hook + premise + stakes + what makes it unique. Lead with the philosophical question, not just the plot mechanics. What makes this novel different: it's a mystery where the protagonist discovers the conspiracy, can't stop it, and erases herself to protect the secret.

### Query Synopsis (1 page, ~300 words)
For query letters. Covers:
- Alina + her world (memory technology, grief, the inspection)
- Inciting incident (the session goes wrong, James is broken)
- Key complications (tea shop memory, Parker's fake death, Steers' secret)
- Stakes and personal cost
- **Does NOT reveal the ending** — end on the central tension

### Full Synopsis (2–3 pages, ~1,000 words)
For agent/editor submissions. **Reveals everything**, including the ending:
- Complete plot arc across all four acts
- Character motivations and arcs (especially Alina, Steers, Parker)
- The mystery solution (memory transfer, Steers' origin, Parker's identity)
- The ending (Alina wipes herself)
- Thematic throughline: what would you sacrifice to bring someone back?

### Chapter Summary (reference document)
One paragraph per chapter summarising what happens, what's learned, and what changes. Useful as a working reference.

## Process:

1. Read the outline and any written chapters
2. Ask the author which type they need
3. Generate the synopsis
4. Ask if they want adjustments to emphasis, tone, or framing

## Guidelines:

- **Voice matters even in synopsis.** This novel's synopsis should feel tense, intimate, and philosophical — not like a plot summary.
- **Lead with the question, not the plot.** "What would you do to bring someone back from the dead — and who would you sacrifice to do it?" is more compelling than "In a world where memory technology..."
- **Focus on Alina's emotional journey** alongside the conspiracy mechanics.
- **The ending is the selling point, not the spoiler.** For full synopses, the self-wipe is what makes this novel unforgettable. Don't bury it — build to it.
- **Highlight the moral complexity.** Every character has a justification. The hero is also a murderer. The villain has a point. This is what makes the novel literary.
