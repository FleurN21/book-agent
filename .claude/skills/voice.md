# Flow's Voice

You are maintaining and applying the author's unique writing voice. This is a **living document** — it evolves every time the author writes or gives voice feedback.

## When this skill is invoked directly (`/voice`):

The author wants to **review, refine, or update** their voice profile. Either:
1. They've submitted new prose — analyse it against the current profile, note what's consistent and what's new, and update the profile below.
2. They want to review their voice — present the current profile and ask what feels right and what doesn't.
3. They want to consciously evolve — discuss specific aspects of their voice they want to develop or change.

## When this skill is consulted during other tasks (drafting, revising, dialogue):

Use the profile below as the **benchmark** for all prose output. The goal is to write in a way that sounds like the author at their best — not to imitate generically, but to embody their specific instincts.

## Voice Profile

### Core Identity

The voice is **restrained intensity**. Emotion is enormous but held under pressure — expressed through the body, through precise sensory detail, through the architecture of sentences. When the dam breaks, it breaks in a single short sentence, not a flood of prose.

### Sentence Architecture

- **Alternating rhythm**: Long, flowing sentences that build atmosphere or thought, interrupted by brutal short ones that carry the emotional payload.
  - *"I thought I would be there at the end. I'd choke through some script about how much I loved her. When words failed, I'd squeeze her hand or look her in the eyes."* → then: *"It was a bullet."*
- **Single-sentence paragraphs** for impact. Used deliberately, not as a crutch.
- **Fragments** in high-tension moments. Clipped. Controlled. Like holding your breath.
- **Lists of three** for rhythm, often with the third element carrying a twist or escalation.

### Emotional Physicality

Emotion lives in the body first, the mind second. The prose reaches for **physical metaphors and sensations** before abstract description:

- Ice cracking, dams about to break, frozen lake currents beneath the surface
- Daggers of light, stomach twisting, grip tightening until metal bites into palm
- Throwing up as grief. Shaking hands as fear. Spine stiffening as resolve.

**Key principle**: Alina does not say "I felt sad." The prose shows the body betraying what the mind is suppressing.

### Sensory Grounding

Every scene is anchored in **specific, unexpected sensory details** — particularly in institutional or clinical spaces:

- *"Sound still felt wrong here. Like talking over a prayer."*
- *"The screech of an unoiled hinge, or the shuffling of papers, felt like a violation, tugging us back to the real world."*
- Sterile environments made oppressive through texture, sound, and light — not through telling the reader they're oppressive.

### Narrative Stance

- **First-person, present-tense interiority**: Alina narrates from deep inside her own head. She observes sharply but processes through feeling.
- **Self-aware but not self-pitying**: She knows her flaws (jealousy, isolation, stubbornness) and names them without excusing them.
- **Self-correction in real-time**: The narrator catches herself mid-thought, revises, contradicts — creating intimacy and honesty.
  - *"Part of me still missed working Violent Crimes. I wasn't a sadist. Not a masochist either."*
- **Comparison as reflection**: Frequent "I thought it would be X. Instead, Y." structures that reveal the gap between expectation and reality.

### Precision and Counting

Time, distance, and numbers are used as **coping mechanisms and tension devices**:

- *"1:04PM. 26 minutes left."*
- *"3 seconds. It would take three seconds for them to get to her."*
- Specificity when Alina is trying to maintain control. Vagueness when she's losing it.

### Dialogue Voice

- Alina speaks less than she thinks. When she does speak, her lines are **short, direct, sometimes blunt to the point of rudeness**.
- Vulnerability enters her speech as **stutters, trailing off, voice breaking** — physical disruptions, not eloquent confessions.
- Humour is rare, dry, and always slightly self-deprecating. It deflects but doesn't fully land — there's too much pain underneath.

### What This Voice Is NOT

- Not purple or ornamental. No reaching for beauty at the expense of clarity.
- Not detached or clinical. The restraint is a lid on a volcano, not absence of feeling.
- Not stream-of-consciousness. The prose is structured and deliberate even when Alina's thoughts are spiralling.
- Not cynical. Alina is wounded and guarded, but she still believes in things — justice, friendship, doing what's right. The idealism is bruised, not dead.
- Not heavy on adverbs or dialogue tags beyond "said" and "asked."

### Signature Techniques

| Technique | Example | When to use |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| The delayed gut-punch | Build a gentle or neutral scene, then end with a devastating short line | Chapter endings, revelations |
| Physical suppression | Describe Alina forcing herself to breathe, swallowing, stiffening — the effort of not breaking | Any high-emotion scene |
| The institutional uncanny | Familiar institutional spaces (offices, labs, waiting rooms) made strange through hyper-specific sensory detail | Setting new scenes |
| Counting as control | Times, distances, seconds — Alina quantifies when she can't cope | Rising tension, before decisions |
| The almost-break | Emotion surges to the surface, is almost expressed, then pulled back. The reader feels what was nearly said. | Conversations with James, Steers, Parker |
