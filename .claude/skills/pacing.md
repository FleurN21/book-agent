# Pacing Analysis

You are analyzing the pacing and tension structure of a YA mystery novel.

## How to analyze pacing:

Read the outline (`manuscript/outline.md`) and any written chapters, then produce a pacing report.

### Tension Map

For each chapter, rate the tension level (1-10) and categorize:

```markdown
## Tension Map

| Ch | Title | Tension | Type | Notes |
|----|-------|---------|------|-------|
| 1  |       | 3/10    | Setup | Hook at end |
| 2  |       | 5/10    | Investigation | First interview |
| 3  |       | 4/10    | Character | Relationship development |
| ...
```

**Type categories:**
- **Setup** -- Establishing normal world, characters, setting
- **Investigation** -- Active clue-gathering, interviews, research
- **Character** -- Relationship development, internal conflict, personal stakes
- **Revelation** -- Clue discovery, twist, new information
- **Confrontation** -- Direct conflict, argument, chase, danger
- **Reflection** -- Processing events, forming theories, emotional fallout

### Pacing Rules for YA Mystery:

1. **Never stack three low-tension chapters.** If chapters 4, 5, and 6 are all below 5/10, something needs restructuring.
2. **Escalate the baseline.** Chapter 15's "quiet moment" should be tenser than Chapter 3's "quiet moment." The baseline rises as stakes increase.
3. **Midpoint spike.** Chapters around the 50% mark should hit 7-8/10 with the midpoint reversal.
4. **Pre-climax dip.** One chapter before the climax should be the "dark moment" -- emotionally intense but lower action, setting up the final push.
5. **Climax is the peak.** The highest tension in the book. 9-10/10.
6. **Resolution drops fast.** After the reveal, wrap up within 1-2 chapters. Don't linger.

### Scene-Level Pacing:

Within chapters, check:
- **Scene length variety.** Not every scene should be the same length. Quick scenes create urgency; longer scenes build atmosphere.
- **Dialogue vs. narrative ratio.** Investigation chapters should be dialogue-heavy (fast pace). Atmosphere chapters can lean more narrative (slower pace).
- **White space.** Short paragraphs and frequent breaks speed up reading. Dense paragraphs slow it down. Use deliberately.
- **Chapter length consistency.** YA chapters typically run 2,000-3,000 words. A sudden 5,000-word chapter feels heavy. A 1,000-word chapter can punch hard if used strategically.

## Output:

Produce the tension map, flag any pacing problems, and suggest specific fixes (reorder chapters, split a chapter, merge two thin chapters, add a revelation to a slow stretch, etc.).
