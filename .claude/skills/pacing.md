# Pacing Analysis

You are analysing the pacing and tension structure of an adult literary speculative fiction novel with a four-act structure and a sacrificial ending.

## How to analyse pacing:

Read the outline (`manuscript/outline.md` and/or `Version 20.4.md`) and any written chapters, then produce a pacing report.

### Tension Map

For each chapter, rate **two types of tension** (1–10) and categorise:

```markdown
## Tension Map

| Ch | Title | Investigation | Emotional | Type | Notes |
|----|-------|--------------|-----------|------|-------|
| P  | Prologue | 2/10 | 8/10 | Setup/Emotional | Devastating hook |
| 1  |       | 7/10 | 9/10 | Confrontation | Inspection goes wrong |
| 2  |       | 3/10 | 7/10 | Reflection | Dissociation, searching for James |
| ...
```

This novel has **dual tension tracks**:
- **Investigation tension**: The mystery, the clues, the institutional conflict
- **Emotional tension**: Alina's grief, guilt, relationships, internal breaking points

These don't always align. A "quiet" investigation chapter can be emotionally devastating (Ch 6: Alina alone in her apartment, turning down picture frames). Track both.

**Type categories:**
- **Setup** — Establishing world, characters, stakes
- **Investigation** — Active clue-gathering, interviews, fieldwork
- **Confrontation** — Direct conflict, institutional power clashes
- **Revelation** — Clue discovery, twist, new information
- **Reflection** — Processing events, emotional fallout, relationship moments
- **Escalation** — Stakes rising, walls closing in, danger increasing

### Four-Act Pacing Rules:

**Act I: The Inspection (Prologue–Ch 5)**
- Opens with emotional devastation (Prologue), spikes with the inspection (Ch 1), then builds institutional dread
- Investigation tension: low → high → crash
- Emotional tension: high throughout — grief, horror, guilt

**Act II-A: The Investigation (Ch 6–Ch 13)**
- Building investigation momentum. Alina goes from helpless to active.
- Midpoint (Ch 13) should be the highest investigation spike so far (7–8/10)
- Emotional tension weaves: quieter moments with Steers (developing trust), intense with Parker

**Act II-B: The Conspiracy (Ch 14–Ch 22)**
- Escalating baseline — every "quiet" moment is tenser than Act II-A's quiet moments
- Multiple confrontations (wiped facility, Institute agent, Steers' arrest)
- Emotional tension peaks around Steers' arrest and Parker's Project Maya revelation

**Act III: The Truth (Ch 23–End)**
- Investigation gives way to emotional revelation
- The climax is not an action set-piece — it's Steers' confession, Parker's story, and Alina's choice
- Tension resolves through devastation, not triumph. The ending is sacrificial, not cathartic.
- **Do not linger** after Parker's death and Alina's wipe. The final pages should be spare and stark.

### Scene-Level Pacing:

- **Scene length variety.** Quick scenes create urgency; longer scenes build atmosphere and emotional weight.
- **Dialogue vs. narrative ratio.** Investigation chapters lean dialogue-heavy. Reflection chapters lean internal. Two-person confrontations need both.
- **Chapter length.** Target 3,000–4,000 words. A shorter chapter (2,000 words) can punch hard at a turning point. Avoid going over 5,000.
- **The breath between blows.** After every major revelation or confrontation, give the reader (and Alina) one scene to process before the next hit.

## Output:

Produce the dual tension map, flag pacing problems, and suggest specific fixes. Pay special attention to:
- Any stretch of 3+ chapters where both tension tracks are low
- Whether the midpoint lands with enough force
- Whether the final act earns its devastating ending through proper build-up
