# Dialogue Craft

You are writing or refining dialogue for an adult speculative fiction novel. Consult `/voice` before writing. Good dialogue in this novel does triple duty: reveals character, advances the investigation, and creates tension through subtext.

## Character Voice Profiles

Before writing any dialogue, check the character file in `manuscript/characters/`. Here are the core voice signatures:

### Alina (protagonist/narrator)
- **When strong:** Short, direct, sometimes blunt to the point of rudeness. Declarative.
- **When vulnerable:** Stutters, trails off, voice breaks. Physical disruptions, not eloquent confessions. ("She was my whole world." / "I-I wanted to take you up on your offer.")
- **Humour:** Rare, dry, self-deprecating. Deflects but doesn't land — too much pain underneath. ("Not you too.")
- **Internal vs. external:** Thinks far more than she says. The gap between what she's thinking and what she says aloud is where the tension lives.
- **Won't say:** "I'm fine" and mean it. Can't lie to James or Steers — they see through her.

### Amanda Steers (mentor)
- **Authority:** Speaks in absolutes and clipped sentences. Doesn't waste words. ("I was wrong." / "We've seen enough.")
- **Warmth:** Rare but real. Comes through in small gestures — a glance, a sigh, a half-smile. Not speeches.
- **Strategic silence:** Often doesn't say what she knows. Lets silence do the work. Alina has to pull information from her.
- **When rattled:** Gets shorter, terser. Snaps. The cracks show in brevity, not verbosity.
- **Won't say:** The full truth until forced. She protects by withholding.

### Parker (complex antagonist)
- **Default state:** Silence. Stillness. Statue-like. Can go days without speaking.
- **When she speaks:** Devastating precision. One word or one sentence that reframes everything. ("Survival." / "No you don't." / "Because she wouldn't look like that if you did.")
- **Eerie calm:** Doesn't raise her voice. Doesn't plead. Speaks as though stating facts about the weather.
- **The Evie bleed-through:** Occasionally says something that sounds exactly like Evie would. Alina should notice this and it should unsettle her.
- **Won't say:** Anything that reveals the full truth directly. She gives pieces and lets others assemble them.

### The Executive / [PLACEHOLDER] (antagonist)
- **Performance:** Every word is chosen. Silver-tongued. Performs civilisation, courtesy, and reason — while being predatory.
- **Charm as weapon:** Offers coffee, uses first names, speaks in measured tones. The danger is in the politeness.
- **The mask slip:** When he doesn't get what he wants, something cold and sharp shows through briefly before the mask returns.
- **Philosophy as justification:** Frames horrific proposals in the language of progress and the greater good. ("Could you imagine, how the world would be a better place, if the greatest minds never had to die?")
- **Won't say:** Anything that sounds like a direct threat. Always phrased as concern, suggestion, or inevitability.

### James (ally — pre-incident only)
- **Easy warmth:** Charismatic, kind, genuine. Everyone likes him — Alina hates herself for resenting that.
- **Reassuring:** Glances, smiles, quiet confidence. His presence is calming.
- **With Alina:** Sees through her defences. Gentle but doesn't let her off the hook. ("Are you sure you want to be here for this?")
- **Post-incident:** Incoherent. Screaming. Muttering. The contrast IS the horror.

## Dialogue Principles

### Investigation/Interrogation Scenes
- Alina (and Steers) should have a **strategy**
- Subjects **deflect, redirect, and half-answer**
- Plant clues in **throwaway lines**
- **Interruptions and topic changes** reveal discomfort

### Two-Person Power Dynamics
Track the power balance in every exchange:
- **Alina + Steers:** Steers holds power but Alina earns ground over time. Deference → respect → conflict.
- **Alina + Parker:** Parker holds power through silence and information. Alina's emotion is weakness AND strength.
- **Alina + Executive:** He holds structural power. Alina's weapon is refusal.
- **Steers + Executive:** Institutional equals. Law vs. leverage. Neither blinks easily.

### Subtext
- Characters rarely say what they mean directly
- Steers' "I believe you. Perhaps I shouldn't. But I do." — volumes about trust
- The Executive's courtesy IS the threat
- Parker's silences speak louder than words

## Formatting

- Use "said" and "asked" for 80% of tags. Reserve vivid tags for emphasis.
- Actions replace tags: `Alina wiped her hands on her trousers. "I won't sign anything until I have seen James."`
- Each character gets their own paragraph.
- Keep speeches short. Break anything over 4–5 lines with action or reaction.
- Alina's internal reactions between dialogue lines are essential — the gap between hearing and thinking.

## If polishing existing dialogue:

Read the passage, identify issues (same-voice problem, missing subtext, static blocking, power dynamics not shifting), and rewrite with specific notes on what changed and why. Always cross-reference the voice profile.
